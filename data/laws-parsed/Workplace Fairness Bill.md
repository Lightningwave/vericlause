## Short title and commencement

1. This Act is the Workplace Fairness Act 2024 and comes into operation on a date that the Minister appoints by notification in the Gazette .

## General interpretation

2. In this Act -
2. 'authorised officer' means any public officer appointed as an authorised officer under section 37(2);
3. 'authorised person' means an individual appointed as an authorised person under section 37(3);
4. 'civil contravention' means a civil contravention under section 29;
5. 'collective agreement' has the meaning given by section 2 of the Industrial Relations Act 1960;
6. 'Commissioner' means the Commissioner for Workplace Fairness appointed under section 37(1), and includes -

## Purposes of Act

3. The purposes of this Act are -
2. ( a ) to protect individuals from discrimination by employers on the ground of protected characteristics;
3. ( b ) to establish fair employment practices;
4. ( c ) to ensure that citizens of Singapore and permanent residents of Singapore are fairly considered for employment opportunities and continue to form the core of the workforce in Singapore, with foreigners as a complement; and

- ( d ) to preserve harmonious workplace relations in Singapore.

## Application of Act

4. -(1) This Act does not apply to the following employers:

- ( a ) (except for section 26) an employer that employs fewer than the prescribed number of individuals;
- ( b ) any prescribed employer or class of employers.

(2) In subsection (1), 'prescribed number' means 25 or any other number that the Minister may, by notification in the Gazette , prescribe in substitution.

(3) To avoid doubt, the following arrangements do not constitute employment:

- ( a ) any contract for service;
- ( b ) any arrangement under a platform work agreement;
- ( c ) any prescribed arrangement or class of arrangements.

## PART 2

## EMPLOYMENT DECISIONS

## Employment decisions - hiring

5. -(1) An employer makes an employment decision if the employer hires or decides not to hire an individual.

- (2) An employer hires an individual if the employer -
2. ( a ) offers to employ the individual (whether or not the offer is accepted); or
3. ( b ) takes any step towards an offer of employment to the individual (whether or not the employer eventually offers to employ the individual), including any of the following steps:
4. (i) inviting the individual to an interview, test or examination for the purposes of possible employment;

## Employment decisions during employment

6. -(1) An employer makes an employment decision during employment if the employer does any of the following:

- ( a ) appraises, evaluates or measures the performance of any employee;
- ( b ) promotes or decides not to promote any employee;
- ( c ) reduces the rank or status of any employee;
- ( d ) provides or does not provide any training to any employee.

(2) For the purposes of subsection (1)( b ), an employer promotes an employee if the employer increases the rank or status of the employee.

(3) For the purposes of subsection (1)( d ), an employer provides training to an employee if the employer provides or facilitates any activity, course, lesson, programme, deployment, attachment or secondment (whether formal or informal) for the purpose of developing or upgrading the employee's skills, expertise or knowledge (whether or not relevant to the employee's job).

## Employment decisions - dismissal, retrenchment and termination

7. -(1) An employer makes an employment decision if the employer dismisses or retrenches, or terminates the contract of service of, an employee.

(2) In subsection (1), 'retrench', in relation to an employee, means to terminate the employee's contract of service at the initiative of the employer because of redundancy or any reorganisation of the employer's profession, business, trade or work.

## PART 4

## DISCRIMINATION

## Discrimination against individuals

17. -(1) It is discrimination for an employer to make an employment decision that adversely affects an individual -

- ( a ) on the ground of a protected characteristic of the individual; or
- ( b ) on the ground of 2 or more reasons, one of which is a protected characteristic of the individual,

unless any of the exceptions in sections 20 to 24 applies in relation to that employer and individual.

(2) For the purposes of subsection (1), it is not necessary to prove that the individual was treated or affected differently compared to any other individual.

(3) An employment decision made only on the ground of a protected characteristic of a relative or an associate of the individual is not discrimination.

(4) For the purposes of subsection (1), a protected characteristic of an individual means -

- ( a ) in the case of a protected characteristic mentioned in section 12 or 13, the protected characteristic -
- (i) as it exists on the day or at the time mentioned in section 12 or 13; or
- (ii) as it is suspected, assumed or believed, by the person making the employment decision, to exist on the day or at the time mentioned in section 12 or 13; or

- ( b ) in any other case, the protected characteristic -
- (i) as it exists at the time the employment decision is made; or
- (ii) as it is suspected, assumed or believed, by the person making the employment decision, to exist at the time the employment decision is made.

## Discrimination by direction, instruction or policy

18. -(1) It is discrimination for an employer to issue, communicate or publish any discriminatory direction, instruction or policy, in writing, unless any of the exceptions in sections 20 to 24 applies in relation to that employer.

(2) In subsection (1), any direction, instruction or policy is discriminatory if it directs, instructs or influences any officer, member, partner, employee or agent of the employer to make, on behalf of the employer, any employment decision that adversely affects an individual, or such employment decisions generally, on the ground of any protected characteristic.

(3) For the purposes of subsection (1), it is immaterial whether the employer in fact makes any employment decision on the ground of a protected characteristic.

## Discrimination by advertisement or description

19. -(1) It is discrimination for an employer to publish in Singapore, or cause to be published in Singapore, an advertisement or description relating to possible employment in Singapore by the employer that mentions (expressly or by implication) a protected characteristic as a condition, criterion, requirement, advantage, disadvantage or disqualification for employment (called in this Act a discriminatory advertisement), unless any of the exceptions in sections 20 to 24 applies in relation to that employer.

(2) In subsection (1), an advertisement or description that is published electronically is treated as published in Singapore if the advertisement or description -

## PART 5

## EXCEPTIONS

## Exception - genuine requirement of job

20. -(1) It is not discrimination for an employer to do any act mentioned in section 17(1), 18(1) or 19(1) on the ground of or in relation to any protected characteristic, if the protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job.

(2) In subsection (1), a protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job if -

- ( a ) having regard to the nature of the job, the job cannot be reasonably performed by an individual unless the individual has (or does not have) the protected characteristic;
- ( b ) the job needs to be performed by an individual who has (or does not have) the protected characteristic to preserve the health or safety of the individual or any other individual;
- ( c ) the job needs to be performed by an individual who has (or does not have) the protected characteristic to preserve standards of privacy (whether reasonable or not) of the individual or any other individual; or
- ( d ) the job must, under any written law or by any requirement imposed under any written law, be performed by an individual who has (or does not have) the protected characteristic.

(3) For the purposes of subsection (2)( a ), the ability to read, speak, write or understand a language is not reasonably necessary for the performance of a job merely because other employees of the employer regularly communicate in that language.

## Exception - age

21. -(1) It is not discrimination for an employer to decide not to hire an individual because the individual is younger than the prescribed age.

## Handling of grievances

27. -(1) An employer must develop a process under which the employer commits in writing to -

- ( a ) inquiring, in a stated manner, into each grievance raised by an employee to the employer;
- ( b ) reviewing, in a stated manner, each grievance raised by an employee to the employer;
- ( c ) informing each employee who has raised a grievance, in a stated manner, of the outcome (if any) of the review mentioned in paragraph ( b );
- ( d ) keeping, for a stated period, a written record of -
- (i) every inquiry mentioned in paragraph ( a ); and
- (ii) every review mentioned in paragraph ( b ); and
- ( e ) not disclosing to any person, unless reasonably necessary -
- (i) the identity of any employee mentioned in paragraph ( a ); or

- (ii) any information in relation to any inquiry mentioned in paragraph ( a ) or any review mentioned in paragraph ( b ).

(2) An employer must inform all its employees in writing of the process mentioned in subsection (1).

## EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to achieve the following main purposes:

- ( a ) to protect individuals from discrimination by employers on the ground of protected characteristics;
- ( b ) to establish fair employment practices;
- ( c ) to ensure that citizens of Singapore and permanent residents of Singapore are fairly considered for employment opportunities and continue to form the core of the workforce in Singapore, with foreigners as a complement;
- ( d ) to preserve harmonious workplace relations in Singapore.

The Bill also makes related amendments to the Employment of Foreign Manpower Act 1990.

The Bill is divided into 11 Parts.

## PART 1

## PRELIMINARY

Part 1 sets out preliminary provisions of the Bill.

Clause 1 relates to the short title and commencement.

Clause 2 is a general interpretation provision. The clause contains definitions of terms used in the Bill, including key terms such as 'discrimination',

'employee', 'employer' and 'employment decision'. The Bill only applies to the Government and statutory authorities in relation to prescribed classes of employees. The Bill does not apply in relation to prescribed employees.

Clause 3 sets out the purposes of the Bill.

Clause 4 provides for the scope of application of the Bill. The Bill, other than clause 26, does not apply to employers that employ fewer than the prescribed number of individuals. The Bill also does not apply to any prescribed employers or class of employers.

## PART 2

## EMPLOYMENT DECISIONS

Part 2 sets out the employment decisions made by employers that come within the scope of the Bill.

Under clause 5, hiring and not hiring are employment decisions. Hiring extends to taking steps toward an offer of employment, including steps such as inviting a candidate for an interview.

Clause 6 deals with employment decisions during employment. An employer makes an employment decision if the employer appraises, evaluates or measures the performance of any employee, promotes or decides not to promote any employee, reduces the rank or status of any employee, or provides or does not provide any training to any employee.

Under clause 7, dismissal, retrenchment and termination of contracts of services are employment decisions.

## PART 3

## PROTECTED CHARACTERISTICS

Part 3 sets out and defines the protected characteristics under the Bill. Generally, a protected characteristic is a characteristic of an individual on the ground of which an employer cannot discriminate when making an employment decision.

Clause 8 sets out the protected characteristics, which are -

- ( a ) age;
- ( b ) nationality;
- ( c ) sex;
- ( d ) marital status;
- ( e ) pregnancy;

- ( f ) caregiving responsibilities;
- ( g ) race;
- ( h ) religion;
- ( i ) language ability;
- ( j ) disability; and
- ( k ) mental health condition.

Clause 9 defines the protected characteristic of nationality. The protected characteristic of nationality refers to an individual's citizenship of any country or an individual's status as a resident of any country (including the status as a permanent resident of Singapore).

Clause 10 defines the protected characteristic of sex. The protected characteristic of sex in relation to an individual is the sex assigned to the individual at birth; or, in the case of an individual who has undergone a sex re-assignment procedure, the sex to which the individual has been re-assigned.

The protected characteristic of sex does not include an individual's sexual orientation or gender identity.

Clause 11 defines the protected characteristic of marital status.

The protected characteristic of marital status includes the following facts:

- ( a ) the fact that the individual is married;
- ( b ) the fact that the individual is not married;
- ( c ) the fact that the individual has been divorced;
- ( d ) the fact that the individual is widowed;
- ( e ) the fact that the individual is married but is living separately from the individual's spouse;
- ( f ) the number of times the individual has married.

Clause 12 defines the protected characteristic of pregnancy. The protected characteristic of pregnancy in relation to a woman on any day means the fact that the woman is pregnant on the day in question or at any time during the prescribed period before the day in question (or at any time, if no period is prescribed); the fact that the woman has expressed a desire or intent to bear children; the fact that the woman breastfeeds an infant or expresses breast milk for the purposes of feeding an infant on the day in question or at any time during the prescribed period before the day in question (or at any time, if no period is prescribed); the fact that the woman is on maternity leave on the day in question or at any time during the prescribed period before the day in question (or at any time, if no period is prescribed); or the fact that the woman (being pregnant on the day in question or at

any time during the prescribed period before the day in question (or at any time, if no period is prescribed)) contracted an illness related to her pregnancy during the pregnancy or in the 6 weeks immediately after the day of her confinement.

Clause 13 defines the protected characteristic of caregiving responsibilities. The protected characteristic of caregiving responsibilities in relation to an individual on any day refers to the individual having a responsibility to care for or support any family member on the day in question or at any time during the prescribed period before the day in question (or at any time, if no period is prescribed). Clause 13 also sets out a definition of 'family member'.

Clause 14 defines the protected characteristic of language ability as the ability of an individual to read, speak, write or understand any language.

Clause 15 defines the protected characteristic of disability. The protected characteristic of disability refers to the fact that an individual has any one or more of the following:

- ( a ) autism;
- ( b ) any intellectual disability;
- ( c ) any physical disability;
- ( d ) any sensory disability.

Clause 16 defines the protected characteristic of mental health condition, which, in relation to an individual, means the fact that the individual has been diagnosed with any mental disorder by a medical practitioner registered under the Medical Registration Act 1997.

## PART 4

## DISCRIMINATION

Part 4 defines discrimination for the purposes of the Bill.

Clause 17(1) provides that it is discrimination for an employer to make an employment decision that adversely affects an individual on the ground of a protected characteristic of the individual, or on the ground of 2 or more reasons, one of which is a protected characteristic of the individual.

'Employment decision' is defined in clause 2 and Part 2.

Under clause 17(3), an employment decision made only on the ground of the protected characteristic of a relative or an associate of an individual (and not the individual himself or herself) is not discrimination.

Under clause 17(4), a protected characteristic includes a characteristic as it is suspected, assumed or believed to exist by the person making the employment decision, whether or not it exists in fact.

To illustrate clause 17, an employer discriminates against a woman ( A ) if the employer dismisses A because A is pregnant. An employer also discriminates against A if the employer dismisses A for many reasons, one of which is that A is pregnant. An employer also discriminates against A if the employer dismisses A because the manager who decided on A 's dismissal thought that A was pregnant, even though A was not in fact pregnant.

However, an employer does not discriminate against A if the employer dismisses A on the ground of the race of A 's husband.

Clause 18 provides for discrimination by a discriminatory direction, instruction or policy, which is when an employer issues, communicates or publishes in writing a direction, instruction or policy relating to employment decisions on the ground of a protected characteristic. An example of such a direction, instruction or policy is a written direction to hiring managers to hire only men. Another example is a written policy in a company handbook stating that pregnant women are not to be selected for a training programme.

Clause 19 deals with discrimination by advertisement or description. It is discrimination for an employer to publish in Singapore, or cause to be published in Singapore, a job advertisement or description that mentions a protected characteristic as a condition, criterion, requirement, advantage, disadvantage or disqualification for employment. An example of such an advertisement or description is a job advertisement stating that persons of a certain race are preferred for the job.

## PART 5

## EXCEPTIONS

Part 5 sets out exceptions where discrimination on the ground of a protected characteristic is not unlawful. These are circumstances where it is not unlawful to discriminate against individuals, by direction, instruction or policy, or by advertisement or description, on the ground of certain protected characteristics.

Clause 20 provides for an exception where a protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job.

Aprotected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job if, having regard to the nature of the job, the job cannot be reasonably performed by an individual unless the individual has (or does not have) the protected characteristic. For example, a job as an audio production manager cannot be reasonably performed by an individual with a hearing impairment. Discrimination against the hearing-impaired individual is not unlawful in relation to the job.

A protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job if the job needs to be performed by an individual who has (or does not have) the protected characteristic to preserve the health or safety of the individual or any other

individual. For example, a job as a high access window cleaner cannot be performed by a woman who is 36 weeks pregnant without endangering the health of the woman. Discrimination on the ground of pregnancy is not unlawful in relation to the job.

A protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job if the job needs to be performed by an individual who is of a particular sex to preserve standards of privacy (whether reasonable or not) of any individual. For example, customers of a spa serving mainly female customers stipulate that all its masseuses should be female to preserve the customers' standards of privacy. Discrimination against men on the ground of sex is not unlawful in relation to the job of a masseuse at the spa.

A protected characteristic is a genuine requirement of a job if any written law or any requirement imposed under any written law requires that the job be performed by an individual who has (or does not have) the protected characteristic. For example, under section 62A of the Road Traffic Act 1961, a person who has attained the prescribed age under that section must not drive a heavy locomotive, light locomotive, motor tractor or heavy motor car. Discrimination against persons who are above that prescribed age is not unlawful in relation to the job of a driver of a heavy locomotive, light locomotive, motor tractor or heavy motor car.

Clause 21 provides for an exception that allows employers to prefer older workers by allowing discrimination in favour of persons older than a prescribed age in hiring, and discrimination against persons younger than that age in hiring. Advertisements, descriptions, directions, instructions and policies in relation to hiring are accordingly permitted to discriminate on that same ground.

Clause 22 provides for an exception that allows discrimination in favour of citizens of Singapore and permanent residents of Singapore.

Clause 23 provides for an exception for religious groups in relation to certain jobs.

Clause 24 provides for an exception that allows discrimination in favour of disabled individuals in hiring, and allows discrimination against abled individuals in hiring. Advertisements, descriptions, directions, instructions and policies in relation to hiring are accordingly permitted to discriminate on that same ground.

## PART 6

## FAIR EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES

Part 6 sets out certain fair employment practices required of employers, namely, in relation to fair consideration of job applications, investigation and review of grievances, and prohibition of retaliation.

Clause 25 sets out the definitions for Part 6.

Clause 26 prohibits employers from applying under section 7(1) of the Employment of Foreign Manpower Act 1990 for a work pass in a prescribed category of work passes if the employer has not fulfilled certain requirements relating to advertising and fair consideration.

Clause 27 requires employers to develop a grievance-handling process under which the employer commits, among other things, to investigating and reviewing all grievances raised by its employees.

Clause 28 prohibits employers from retaliating against an employee (by taking certain retaliatory actions set out in clause 28(2)) because the employee has, among other things, brought proceedings against the employer under the Bill or given evidence or information against the employer under the Bill.

## PART 7

## PENALTIES AND DIRECTIONS

Part 7 provides for penalties and directions under the Bill.

Under clause 29, generally, acts of discrimination under Part 4 and contraventions of fair employment practices under Part 6 are civil contraventions.

Under clause 30, certain civil contraventions are serious civil contraventions.

There are generally 3 penalties that may result from a civil contravention.

First, an administrative financial penalty may be imposed by way of a contravention notice issued under clause 31. Reconsideration of a contravention notice may be requested under clause 32(1), and an appeal to the General Division of the High Court may also be made under clause 32(4). A contravention notice may be enforced against an employer, with the permission of a District Court, in the same manner as a judgment of that court.

Second, a direction may be issued to an employer under clause 33 in lieu of or in addition to an administrative financial penalty. Failure to comply with such a direction, without a reasonable excuse, is an offence.

Third, for serious civil contraventions, the Commissioner for Workplace Fairness (the Commissioner) may, with the consent of the Public Prosecutor, bring an action in court to seek an order for a civil penalty under clause 34. Under clause 35, such an action cannot commence after the expiry of 6 years from the date of the serious civil contravention or if an administrative financial penalty has been imposed for an act that relates to the serious civil contravention.

Clause 36 provides that individual officers, members or partners of contravening employers who are bodies corporate, partnerships or unincorporated associations are to be treated as having committed the

contravention if the contravention is proved to have been committed with the consent or connivance of the individual. Action may accordingly be taken against the individual under clause 31, 33 or 34.

## PART 8

## ADMINISTRATION AND INVESTIGATIONS

Part 8 provides for the administration and investigations under the Bill.

Clause 37 provides for the appointment by the Minister of the Commissioner for Workplace Fairness, as well as Deputy Commissioners for Workplace Fairness, Principal Assistant Commissioners for Workplace Fairness and Assistant Commissioners for Workplace Fairness. The Minister may also appoint any public officer as an authorised officer, and any individual as an authorised person.

Clause 38 provides for the investigative powers of the Commissioner (including Deputy Commissioners for Workplace Fairness, Principal Assistant Commissioners for Workplace Fairness and Assistant Commissioners for Workplace Fairness), authorised officers and authorised persons.

Clause 39 provides for the protection of the Commissioner, authorised officers and authorised persons from personal liability in their performance of functions under the Bill in good faith and with reasonable care.

## PART 9

## MISCELLANEOUS

Part 9 sets out miscellaneous provisions.

Clause 40 provides for protection for complainants from liability for breaches of any code of professional etiquette or ethics, from civil and criminal liability, and from identification by witnesses in proceedings under the Bill.

Clause 41 voids any term of a contract of service or collective agreement that excludes or limits the operation of any provision of the Bill, or precludes any person from making a complaint or allegation or raising a grievance under the Bill.

Clause 42 provides for the composition of offences.

Clause 43 provides that the Minister may exempt any person or class of persons.

Clause 44 empowers the Minister to make regulations.

## PART 10

## RELATED AMENDMENTS

Part 10 consists of clause 45, which makes related amendments to the Employment of Foreign Manpower Act 1990 to provide that the Controller of Work Passes under that Act may refuse to issue a work pass if the applicant has not complied with the fair consideration requirements under clause 26(1).

## PART 11

## SAVING AND TRANSITIONAL PROVISION

Part 11 consists of clause 46, which empowers the Minister to make regulations to prescribe any provisions of a saving or transitional nature consequent on the enactment of any provision of the Bill for a period of 2 years after the date of commencement of the provision.

## EXPENDITURE OF PUBLIC MONEY

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure, the exact amount of which cannot at present be ascertained.
